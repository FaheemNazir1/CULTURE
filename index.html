Welcome to India's State-Wise Heritage

"Explore the unique cultural and historical heritage of each Indian state, rich in traditions and monuments."

Heritage of Indian States

Andhra Pradesh

Known for the ancient city of Amaravati, and the grand Tirupati Balaji temple.

Arunachal Pradesh

The beautiful Tawang Monastery and natural beauty of the Eastern Himalayas.

Assam

Famous for the Kaziranga National Park and the Ahom Kingdom's capital, Sivasagar.

Bihar

Home to the ancient Nalanda University and Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Chhattisgarh

Rich with tribal heritage and the historical site of Sirpur.

Goa

Famous for its Portuguese architecture and the Basilica of Bom Jesus.

Gujarat

Known for the Sun Temple of Modhera and the Rann of Kutch.

Haryana

Kurukshetra, the site of the Mahabharata war, and historical Harappan ruins.

Himachal Pradesh

Known for its Buddhist monasteries and the heritage village of Pragpur.

Jharkhand

Ranchi, with its temples and waterfalls, and the Deoghar Baidyanath temple.

Karnataka

Home to the ancient ruins of Hampi and the Mysore Palace.

Kerala

Famous for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the backwaters of Alappuzha.

Madhya Pradesh

Known for the Khajuraho temples and the ancient city of Ujjain.

Maharashtra

Home to the Ajanta and Ellora caves, and the Gateway of India in Mumbai.

Manipur

Known for the Loktak Lake and the famous Kangla Fort.

Meghalaya

Home to the Living Root Bridges and the sacred forests of Mawphlang.

Mizoram

Rich in tribal heritage and the scenic Phawngpui National Park.

Nagaland

Famous for the Hornbill Festival and the World War II Kohima Cemetery.

Odisha

Home to the Konark Sun Temple and the Jagannath Temple in Puri.

Punjab

The Golden Temple in Amritsar and the heritage of the Sikh Empire.

Rajasthan

Known for the forts of Jaipur and Udaipur, and the desert city of Jaisalmer.

Sikkim

Famous for its monasteries like Rumtek and the beauty of Tsomgo Lake.

Tamil Nadu

Home to the grand temples of Madurai and the UNESCO-listed Brihadeeswarar Temple.

Telangana

Known for the historic Charminar and the Golconda Fort.

Tripura

Famous for the Ujjayanta Palace and the Neermahal Water Palace.

Uttar Pradesh

The land of the Taj Mahal, Fatehpur Sikri, and the ancient city of Varanasi.

Uttarakhand

Known for its religious sites like Haridwar and Rishikesh, and the Valley of Flowers.

West Bengal

Famous for the Victoria Memorial in Kolkata and the Sundarbans mangroves.
